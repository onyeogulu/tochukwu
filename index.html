RESEARCH

MAESTRO Jr - Multi-sensing AI Environment for Surgical Task & Role Optimisation

Principal Investigators: Prof. Fabio Cuzzolin & Dr. George Mylonas.

MAESTRO Jr is a collaborative project between Imperial College London and Oxford Brookes University, funded by the UKRI. The project aims to develop a multi-sensing AI environment for surgical task and role optimisation. The project will leverage advanced machine learning techniques to analyse data from various sensors in the operating room, with the goal of improving surgical outcomes and enhancing the efficiency of surgical teams.

During my MSc. program, I worked on this project as a research assistant involved in the development of machine learning algorithms for the analysis of sensor data, as well as the design and implementation of experiments to validate the effectiveness of these algorithms in real-world surgical settings.

About

I am a PhD student specializing in behavioural genetics, with a focus on computational approaches. My research integrates machine learning, deep learning, and health data science to explore how genetic factors influence behavior. Skilled in AI and bioinformatics, I analyze complex datasets to identify genetic markers associated with various behavioral traits. My work aims to bridge the gap between genetic predispositions and observable behaviors, contributing to our understanding of the genetic basis of human behavior through innovative research methodologies. By employing advanced computational tools, I strive to uncover insights that can inform personalized medicine, psychological interventions, and public health strategies.
